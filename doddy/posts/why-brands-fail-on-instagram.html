Why most brands fail on Instagram

D
By Doddy
Apr 5, 2026 • 5 min read

Every D2C founder I know blames the Instagram algorithm. It's never the algorithm. It's that the content is polished, safe, and completely forgettable.

The scroll test

Open your feed right now. Scroll for 30 seconds. How many brand posts did you stop on? How many did you remember an hour later? That's the bar. Your content has to pass it, or it doesn't exist.

Instagram isn't a broadcast channel. It's a fight for half a second of attention, ten times a day, against comedians and cats.

What doesn't work

  • Product on a white background with a caption that starts "Introducing…"
  • Aesthetic flat-lays that look like every other aesthetic flat-lay
  • Quote posts in your brand font on your brand color
  • "New launch coming soon 👀" with no reason to care

None of these are bad. They're just invisible.

What works

Three patterns, all stolen from creators, not brands:

1. Build in public

Show the unpolished parts. The wrong label print. The bottle that leaked. The supplier who ghosted you. People don't follow brands — they follow humans. Give them a human to follow.

2. Hook in the first second

The first frame of a Reel or the first word of a caption decides whether anyone sees the rest. "Here's why your cologne makes your date cringe" beats "Our new fragrance launches today" every single time.

3. Pattern-break the visual

If everyone in your niche shoots moody black-and-white, you shoot in daylight with acid green text. If everyone films Reels in their bedroom, you film in a metro station. The algorithm doesn't reward beauty — it rewards watch time, and novelty drives watch time.

The meta-point

Brands fail on Instagram because they're trying to look like brands. The ones that win look like someone you'd want to text.

Start there. The algorithm takes care of itself.
