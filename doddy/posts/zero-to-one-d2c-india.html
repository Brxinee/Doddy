Zero to one, solo, in India

D
By Doddy
Apr 10, 2026 • 6 min read

A year ago, I had a wordmark, a ₹15,000 budget, and no co-founder. Today the brand is pre-launch with a finished product, a site, and people actually asking when it ships. Here's what I got wrong, and what finally started working.

What I got wrong

I thought the product was the hard part. Formula, bottle, label, manufacturing — I burned three months obsessing over a 50ml mist while nobody on the internet knew the brand existed.

The truth: your first product is a coin flip. What's not a coin flip is whether anyone is listening when you launch it.

A product in a silent room doesn't exist. A bad product in a loud room gets feedback.

The supplier trap

I spent weeks calling ibuychemikals, Medi Plastics in Bowenpally, and a fragrance house in Balanagar. Every call made me feel productive. None of them moved the business forward until I had demand. Suppliers are a solved problem — build for 50 units, scale when it sells.

→ Lesson

If you're pre-launch and spending more than 20% of your week on suppliers, you're procrastinating on marketing. Every time.

What started working

Three things, in order of impact:

  1. Building in public on Instagram — one Reel a day in Hinglish, showing the unsexy parts. Labels being revised. Bottle samples arriving. It doesn't go viral, but it builds a 200-person audience that actually cares.
  2. A landing page with one job — collect emails. Not explain the product, not list features. One hero, one CTA. Conversion tripled.
  3. Talking to real humans — DMing 20 gym-goers and office workers a day, asking what they use for clothes smelling stale. 18 out of 20 said nothing. That's the whole product thesis, validated in two days of messages.

The math that matters

₹200/day on Meta Ads with a ₹159 product means you need a CAC < ₹100 to survive D2C. In India, for a new brand, that's nearly impossible in month one. So the real game is Amazon first (borrow their trust), reviews second, D2C third.

I flipped this order twice before figuring it out.

What's next

Final product photos, barcode from GS1 India, Amazon FBM listing, then the first 50 units go live. Everything else — the site, the deck, the ads — is already built. That's a good problem to have.
