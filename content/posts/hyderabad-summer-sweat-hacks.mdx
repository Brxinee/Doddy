I live in Hyderabad. I started Smelloff here. And I can tell you that the first time you walk from Ameerpet metro to a meeting in Begumpet at 2pm in May, no amount of imported French deodorant prepares you for what your body is about to do.

Hyderabad summer is not a season. It\'s a personality test. Forty-three degrees, dry early in the season then humid once the pre-monsoon arrives, and a sun that has personal beef with you. The grooming advice that works in London or even Bangalore does not survive contact with this weather. Here's what actually works — the stuff I\'ve figured out the hard way over multiple summers, not the listicle version.

## 1. Apply antiperspirant the night before

This is the single biggest unlock and almost nobody does it. The active ingredients in antiperspirant need time and dry skin to form the temporary plugs in your sweat ducts. If you apply it in the morning to skin that's still slightly damp from a shower, and then you immediately walk out into the heat and start sweating, the product gets washed out before it can do its job.

Apply at night, after a shower, to fully dry underarms. Sleep on it. In the morning you\'ll already be ahead of the day. You can re-apply in the morning too if you want, but the night application is the one that does the heavy lifting.

## 2. Switch to lighter fabrics — and actually pay attention to the weave

Cotton is good. Loose cotton is better. Linen is best for the worst weeks. But the thing nobody tells you is that the *weave* matters as much as the fiber. A tight cotton weave traps heat and sweat against your body. A loose weave lets air move. If you can see a faint amount of light through the fabric when you hold it up, it\'ll breathe.

Polyester shirts marketed as "sport" or "dri-fit" are great for actual sport. They are terrible for sitting in an office because they don't actually move sweat away — they just stop absorbing it, which means it pools on your skin and stays there.

## 3. Carry a small towel. Seriously.

I know it sounds desi-uncle. It is desi-uncle. The desi uncles were right.

A small cotton towel — the kind that fits in a backpack pocket — is the difference between arriving at a meeting damp and arriving at a meeting dry. Step into a bathroom for thirty seconds, blot your face and the back of your neck, and you reset. No amount of body spray does what blotting does. The body spray is treating the symptom. The towel is removing the cause.

## 4. Drink water at room temperature, not cold

Counter-intuitive but real. Ice-cold water tells your body it's hot and you need to cool down — which makes you sweat more once the cold sensation passes. Room-temperature water keeps your core temperature steadier and you sweat less. This is one of those things every Hyderabadi grandmother already knows but every twenty-something city guy ignores until he tries it.

## 5. Two t-shirts a day is not a luxury, it's strategy

If you're leaving the house at 9am and not getting home until 9pm, especially if you're commuting on a bike or in autos, carry a second t-shirt in your bag. Change at lunchtime in the office bathroom. The first shirt is now soaked with twelve hours\' worth of bacterial breeding ground. The second shirt is fresh. You will feel like a different person for the second half of the day.

This sounds extra. It\'s not. It\'s the single biggest difference between guys who look composed at 6pm and guys who look like they\'ve been through a war.

## 6. Wash workout clothes immediately, not in two days

Gym clothes get the worst of it. Sweat, friction, heat, synthetic fabric. If you toss them in a laundry pile and let them sit for two days before washing, the bacteria multiply exponentially and the smell sets into the fibers permanently. By "permanently" I mean: even after a hot wash, the shirt will smell faintly of last week\'s workout for the rest of its life.

Rinse them in cold water as soon as you get home. Let them air-dry. Wash them properly within 24 hours. This single habit will extend the life of your gym wardrobe by months.

## 7. Address the shirt smell, not just the body smell

This is the one I keep coming back to in every post on this blog because it's the one Indian men get wrong most often. Once you\'ve done everything right — antiperspirant, breathable fabric, towel, water, change of clothes — there\'s still the question of the shirt you're currently wearing. If you\'ve been in it for six hours in Hyderabad heat, it has a smell. Not because you're unhygienic, but because that's what fabric does in this climate.

Body spray will mask that smell for ten minutes. Cologne will sit on top of it and mix with it badly. The actual fix is something applied directly to the fabric that neutralizes the odor compounds in the fibers.
