About

Who I am

I'm Brainee. I live in Hyderabad and I run Smelloff — a fabric odor eliminator brand I built from scratch, solo. No co-founder, no agency, no funding round. Just a real problem I found, a product I formulated, and a business I'm figuring out as I go.

The product is ODORSTRIKE-based fabric mist. It neutralizes odors on clothing at a molecular level rather than masking them. ₹159. One SKU. It does one thing and it does it properly.

Before Smelloff I worked in design and spent a lot of time around brands without making one. This is me making one.

What Doddy is

Doddy is where I write to think in public. Not a media property. Not a content strategy. A notebook I'm leaving open.

The useful version of founder writing isn't the retrospective (written after the exit with 20/20 hindsight) or the hustle-porn (wake at 4am, here's my morning routine). It's the stuff written from inside the process — while the decisions are being made, when there's still uncertainty, when the answer isn't clear yet.

That's what I'm trying to do here. Write from inside the build. Share the reasoning, not just the result.

The name Doddy is a nickname. It doesn't mean anything clever. It just felt right for something personal.

What I write about

  • Building Smelloff — the real story of D2C in India. Pricing decisions, packaging choices, what acquisition looked like in month one, what changed by month three. The parts that normally get cleaned up before publication.
  • Design — I do the visual identity for Smelloff myself. Matte black, acid green, Barlow Condensed on the label. I want to document those choices: what I was thinking, what I was reacting against, what the principle was. Design is mostly decisions, not talent.
  • Marketing experiments — What works for a single-product D2C brand when you don't have budget for performance marketing. Organic, SEO, Instagram, the economics of product photography. Things I'm testing and what I learned.
  • Notes to self — Everything else. Books that shifted something. Ideas that don't fit a category. Observations about building solo that feel worth writing down.
