I almost didn't do this.

Blogs feel like something from 2009. Everyone's on Twitter, or making Reels, or doing newsletters through Substack. Starting a plain-text blog in 2026 feels slightly absurd. Which is probably why I'm doing it.

Here's the honest version: I've been building Smelloff for a while now, and most of what I'm learning has nowhere to go. I'll figure something out — about pricing, or packaging, or why a product photo converted better than another — and I'll just sit with it. It disappears. Or I'll tell someone at a coffee shop and they'll nod politely while clearly not caring.

Writing forces me to actually think. Not just feel like I'm thinking. There's a difference. When I'm talking through an idea, I can bullshit myself in real time. When I'm writing, I have to finish sentences. I have to know what I actually mean.

So Doddy is partly just a thinking tool that happens to be public.

The name is simple. Doddy is a nickname that stuck. It's not a brand. It's not a content strategy. It's a name I've had since school and I didn't want to think too hard about this.

**What I'll write about:**

Building Smelloff — the actual decisions, not the cleaned-up version. Why I chose a certain price point, what a product launch looked like from the inside, what went wrong and what I'd repeat.

Design — I do the Smelloff design work myself. That's unusual for a D2C founder and I want to write about why I made that choice and what it looks like in practice. Design is mostly decisions, not talent, and I want to document mine.

Whatever I'm figuring out — sometimes that's marketing, sometimes it's how to stay focused while running something alone, sometimes it's a book I read that shifted something.

I'm not writing for an audience per se. If people read it, that's good. If not, I'll have a decent log of how I thought about things during this stretch of building. Both outcomes are fine.

No monetisation plans. No "content calendar." No posting schedule I'll abandon in three weeks. Just writing when something's worth writing about.

If you want to follow along, there's an RSS feed. If you want to email me, xbrainee@gmail.com. I read everything.

Let's see where this goes.
